# Faceless Budget Anomaly Shorts – Video Format & Strategy

## Viral Template Analysis
- Hook (first 3–5s): Bold text overlay with shocking claim, stock/map background, upbeat music.
- Countdown list (#5→#1): Each entry uses:
  - Number label animation (#3, #2, etc.)
  - Text overlay (3–6 words, bold)
  - Narration (AI/TTS voice, short sentences)
  - Transition sound (ding/swoosh)
  - Background: stock clips or simple charts/maps
- Visual Style: Consistent font/color, smooth transitions, captions on-screen.
- Audio: Royalty-free looped track, clear AI narration, volume ducking under VO.
- Ending CTA: Strong “Follow for more / Comment your guess” overlay.

## Observations from Reference Video
- Automation-friendly: Stock footage, template transitions → easily replicable.
- Engagement-first: Simplifies data into eye-catching 1-liners.
- Template-based: Fonts, timing, pacing are uniform (batch-generated).
- Optimal length: 30–45s (TikTok/Shorts sweet spot).

## Adaptation to Budget Anomaly Content
- Hook: “Indiana’s counties are spending WILD this year 🚨”
- Countdown Entries:
  - Example: “#3: Monroe County – +80% Roads Budget”
  - Narration: “Monroe County saw an 80% spike in road spending this year.”
  - Visuals: Line/bar chart overlay highlighting the spike, county map highlight.
- Transition FX: Ding/swoosh between entries.
- CTA: “Should taxpayers be worried? Comment below 👇” + “Follow for hidden spending”

## Why It Works for Budget Watchdog
- Direct mapping of anomalies → ranked list format.
- DOGEGPT pipeline outputs (county, dept, % change) → fits directly into video template.
- Visual uniqueness: Using real anomaly charts/maps makes channel stand out from generic “city IQ” videos.
- Fully automatable: pipeline → script → AI narration → CapCut/InVideo template → upload.
